The Starting Point

Five years ago you filed the paperwork. That part is already done, and it counts for more than it feels like right now. What VTREI doesn't have yet is a financial history — a track record that banks, bureaus, landlords, and dealerships can read the way they read a person's credit score. That's the entire project from here: give the entity a history.

This guide is built around two timelines running side by side. One is the truth about how fast business credit actually moves. The other is what you can put in motion this week so that six months from now, the truth has moved a lot further than it would have if you'd waited.

Setting Real Expectations

What's actually possible, and when

You asked for the apartment, the car, the home, the Airbnb — inside a month or two, on the LLC alone. Here's the honest shape of that timeline, so nothing on this list surprises you later.

Weeks 1 – 4Foundation
Business bank account, EIN documentation, D-U-N-S number, and your first vendor account can all be live. No lender will treat the LLC as a stand-alone borrower yet — there's no history to check.
Months 2 – 6First trade lines
Net-30 vendor accounts start reporting. A secured business credit card can be open and paid down monthly. An Airbnb booked under the business, or a car lease with a personal guarantee, becomes realistic — the LLC's name is on it, your signature backs it.
Year 1 – 2Real trade history
A PAYDEX score exists. Business credit unions and regional banks will talk to you about a line of credit tied to the entity. An apartment lease under the LLC is negotiable with a landlord, particularly with revenue or a strong guarantor picture behind it.
Year 2 – 3+Independent standing
This is where an LLC can begin to borrow largely on its own history — auto financing, a DSCR or commercial loan for property, vendor terms with no personal guarantee. This is the "personal credit fades into the background" phase — and it's earned, not skipped.
NOTE I'm not a lawyer or a financial advisor, and this isn't a loan pre-approval — it's a map. Confirm specifics with a CPA, a business banker, and the actual landlord, dealer, or lender you're working with, since terms vary by state and by institution.
The Question You Actually Asked

Should you file, even at $0 in revenue?

✓ Yes — file. Every year, on time.

It isn't a flex, and it isn't a confession. It's maintenance. A $0 return does three specific things for you:

It keeps VTREI legally alive. States dissolve LLCs that go quiet on their filings. A dissolved LLC has no credit history to build on top of.

It builds the paper trail lenders check. Consistent filings, even at zero, tell a bank the entity is real and current — the opposite of a shell that appeared last week to dodge personal credit checks.

It's required, in most structures, regardless of income. If VTREI is taxed as a sole proprietorship or partnership, a Schedule C or Form 1065 is likely still due. Confirm the exact form with a CPA — it takes one conversation.

The Sequence

Six moves, in order

Each step below unlocks the one after it. Skipping ahead — applying for a car lease before the business bank account exists, for instance — is the most common way people stall out at month one.

1
This week
Open a dedicated business bank account
Use the EIN, not your SSN, to open it. Nothing downstream works without this — it's the account every vendor, bureau, and future lender will eventually ask to see statements from.
  • Bring your Articles of Organization, EIN letter, and operating agreement if you have one
  • A local credit union's business banker is often faster and more relationship-minded than a national bank branch
2
Week 1 – 2
Register the entity's identity
Get a free D-U-N-S number from Dun & Bradstreet — this is how business bureaus recognize VTREI as an entity distinct from you personally. Confirm your Arizona LLC filing is current and in good standing.
  • D-U-N-S is free; expedited processing is a paid option if you don't want to wait ~30 days
  • Consider a dedicated business address and phone line if you can — it strengthens how "operational" the entity looks to bureaus
3
Week 2 – 4
File the $0 return
Close the compliance gap from the last five years if there is one, then get current. A CPA can tell you in one sitting exactly what's owed and what isn't.
4
Month 1 – 3
Open your first trade lines
Net-30 vendor accounts are the fastest legitimate way to start a reportable payment history under the EIN. Buy something small, pay it in full and on time, and it reports.
  • Uline, Grainger, Quill, and Summa Office Supplies are commonly used starter vendors
  • Pair this with a secured business credit card — Ramp and Divvy are common starting points for young LLCs
5
Month 2 – 4
Register with the business bureaus
Dun & Bradstreet (PAYDEX), Experian Business, and Equifax Business are where your vendor payments actually accumulate into a score. Your personal bureaus never see this activity — which is exactly the separation you're after.
6
Ongoing, from month 3 on
Build the credit union relationship
Sit down — in person if you can — with a business banking officer, not the teller line. Ask directly what a young, pre-revenue LLC needs to qualify for a small secured line of credit. Credit unions are more likely to grow with you as the trade history builds.
"The five-year-old filing isn't wasted time — it's the entity's birth certificate. Everything from here is teaching the world to trust it."

Grounding the Plan

The five W's — answered plainly

You asked for these, so here they are — pointed at the thing you're actually building, not at everything at once.

Who
Who's using this infrastructure?
You, as Founder & CEO of VTREI — plus every future landlord, lender, and vendor who reads the entity's history instead of your personal one.
What
What kind of LLC is this?
A single-member Arizona LLC, five years registered, EIN active, currently pre-revenue — which just means it's early, not that it's behind.
When
When does this start paying off?
Movement is visible within weeks. Meaningful leverage — leases, loans, larger lines — is a one-to-three-year build. Both are true at once.
Where
Where do you start?
The business bank account, this week. Everything else in this guide sits downstream of that one account.
Why
Why do it in order, instead of all at once?
Because lenders read sequence as credibility. An entity with a bank account, a filing history, and three months of paid vendor invoices reads as real. The same entity applying for a car lease with none of that reads as risk — and gets a personal-guarantee answer either way. The order isn't bureaucracy for its own sake; it's what actually gets you to "yes" faster.
